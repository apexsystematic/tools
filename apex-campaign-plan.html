01
Step-by-Step Setup Plan
Week-by-week execution — what to build, when, in what order
1
Week 1 — Foundation ~3 hrs total
Lock your LinkedIn profile as a landing page
Before any outreach or posting, your profile must do the selling. Rewrite your headline from a job title to a outcome statement: "I build AI automations that save law firms and IFAs 10+ hours a week". Rewrite your About section: one paragraph on who you help, one on what you do specifically, one CTA (book a call link). Add your two niche landing page URLs in the Featured section once they're live. This is the single highest-ROI hour you'll spend.
2
Week 1 — Foundation ~2 hrs total
Build both niche landing pages with AI
Use Claude to generate the full copy for both pages (law firm + IFA) using the outlines in Section 02. Deploy on Apex Systematic as subpages or standalone. Each page needs one booking link — use Cal.com with a 20-minute "Discovery Call" slot. Do not overthink design. Clean, specific, and fast-loading beats beautiful and slow.
3
Week 1 — Foundation 1 hr setup
Set up your outreach tracking system
Create a simple Airtable base with columns: Name, Firm, LinkedIn URL, Date Connected, Follow-up Date, Status (connected / messaged / replied / call booked / dead). You need to be able to see your pipeline at a glance. Nothing more complex than this. Add 20 target firms before you send a single message.
4
Weeks 2–4 — Ongoing 30 min/day
Post on LinkedIn 3–4x per week
Use the 30-day schedule in Section 03. Batch-draft a week of posts every Sunday using Claude — takes 30 minutes. Edit for your voice, schedule using LinkedIn's native scheduler or Buffer. The format that works best for B2B service selling: short punchy hook (1–2 lines), 3–5 lines of substance, one clear takeaway or CTA. No long essays until you have an audience.
5
Weeks 2–4 — Ongoing 2–3 hrs/week
Run cold outreach: 20 new firms per week
Monday: identify and add 20 new targets to Airtable. Tuesday–Thursday: send connection requests (no pitch, just relevance). Friday: follow up with anyone who accepted earlier that week. Use the message templates in Section 04. Never pitch on the first message. The goal of message one is a connection. The goal of message two is a reply. The goal of message three is a call.
6
Daily — 10 min 10 min/day
Engage on LinkedIn every day
Leave 3–5 substantive comments per day on posts by people in your target niches (law firm partners, IFA principals, compliance officers). Not "great post!" — a single genuine sentence that adds something. This gets your name visible to their network without posting. It compounds fast. Do it before you check anything else.
7
End of Month 1 hr review
Review, cut what's not working, double what is
Check: Which LinkedIn posts got the most engagement? Do more of that format. Which outreach message variant got the most replies? Standardise on it. How many calls booked? If zero — your message or targeting is off, not your service. Adjust one variable at a time. By day 30 you should have at minimum 5–10 active conversations and 1–3 calls booked.

30-Day LinkedIn Schedule
4 posts/week · Mon, Tue, Thu, Fri · Batch-draft on Sundays with AI
Week 1 Establish the problem
Monday · Day 1
Pain Agitation
"Law firms lose an average of 3 billable hours per day to admin. Here's where it goes."
List the 5 biggest time drains. No solution yet — just name the problem. End with: "Any of these sound familiar?"
Tuesday · Day 2
Educational
"What actually is an AI automation agency? (Not what you think)"
Explain what an AAA does in plain language. Differentiate from software vendors and IT consultants. End with what makes your approach different.
Thursday · Day 4
Pain Agitation
"Your IFA firm is answering the same 6 questions 40 times a week."
List the questions. Point out the cost in time. Tease that there's a better way — no pitch, just curiosity hook.
Friday · Day 5
Authority / Story
"I came from operations, not software. Here's why that matters for automation."
Tell your background briefly. Explain why operational experience spots the real bottlenecks. Builds trust and differentiates from tech-first agencies.
Week 2 Introduce solutions
Monday · Day 8
Educational
"How a law firm chatbot actually works — step by step."
Walk through a real intake flow: visitor lands → chatbot qualifies → data goes to CRM → lawyer gets a summary. Make it concrete, not abstract.
Tuesday · Day 9
Pain Agitation
"The hidden cost of a delayed lead response (the numbers are worse than you think)."
Reference the stat that response time under 5 minutes vs 30 minutes dramatically affects conversion. Apply to law/IFA context.
Thursday · Day 11
Educational
"Make.com vs Zapier — which one is right for your firm?"
Quick honest comparison. Position yourself as the expert who knows both. This ranks well and gets saved/shared by people researching.
Friday · Day 12
Soft Offer
"I'm offering 5 free workflow audits this month for law firms or IFA practices."
20-minute call, identify the one automation that would save the most time, no pitch. DM you or book via link. First direct offer of the month.
Week 3 Build credibility
Monday · Day 15
Authority / Breakdown
"I mapped out a typical IFA's week. 11 hours were pure admin. Here's the breakdown."
Create a fictional but realistic time audit. Show which tasks are automatable. This is highly shareable in IFA communities.
Tuesday · Day 16
Educational
"The 3 automations every law firm should have in place before they scale."
Client intake, follow-up sequences, document workflow. Explain each in 2–3 sentences. End with: "Most firms have zero of these."
Thursday · Day 18
Pain Agitation
"Why 'we've always done it this way' is costing your firm £X per month."
Calculate the cost of manual tasks at a typical hourly rate. Keep it conservative and credible. Numbers make people stop scrolling.
Friday · Day 19
Authority / Opinion
"Unpopular opinion: most small firms don't need custom software. They need better workflows."
Contrast expensive software builds vs. no-code automation. Position your approach as the pragmatic, faster option. Good for engagement/debate.
Week 4 Convert and close
Monday · Day 22
Educational
"What a client onboarding automation looks like in practice — from first enquiry to signed contract."
Walk through a full end-to-end flow. Specific steps, specific tools. The more concrete, the more trust it builds.
Tuesday · Day 23
Authority / Results
"What 30 days of building automations for professional services taught me."
Honest reflection post. What surprised you. What worked. What didn't. Human, specific, non-salesy. Best trust-builder of the month.
Thursday · Day 25
Pain Agitation
"If your team is manually entering leads into your CRM, you're already behind."
Short, punchy. Describe the manual process, the errors it causes, the time it wastes. End with: "This is a one-day fix."
Friday · Day 26
Direct Offer
"I have 2 project spots open in May. Here's what working with me looks like."
Describe your process: audit call → scoped proposal → fixed-price build → handover. Who it's for. How to start. Clear, confident, no desperation.
04
Cold Outreach Strategy
How to find firms, who to target, what to say
Finding Law Firms
LinkedIn Search Method
1
Search LinkedIn for "solicitor" OR "partner" OR "managing partner" — filter by your target region
2
Narrow by firm size: 11–50 employees (small enough to feel the admin pain, big enough to have budget)
3
Target personal injury, family law, conveyancing, employment law — high-volume enquiry practices with repetitive intake
4
Also use The Law Society's Find a Solicitor directory — search by practice area, find the firm, find the principal on LinkedIn
5
Target the managing partner or practice manager — not a junior. They feel the operational pain and have the budget sign-off
Finding IFAs
LinkedIn + FCA Register Method
1
Search LinkedIn for "independent financial advisor" OR "IFA" OR "financial planner"
2
Filter by company size 1–10 or 11–50 — these are the firms with no dedicated ops team where admin pain is highest
3
Cross-reference with the FCA Financial Services Register (register.fca.org.uk) — search by firm type, get firm names, then find principals on LinkedIn
4
Also check VouchedFor and Unbiased — IFA directories where you can identify active, client-facing firms
5
Target the firm principal or director — in small IFA practices, this person is both the advisor and the ops manager
Message Sequence — Law Firms
Message 1 — Connection Request (300 char limit)
Hi [First Name] — I work specifically with law firms on automating client intake and admin workflows. Saw you're at [Firm Name] — would be good to connect.
Message 2 — After They Accept (send 2–3 days later)
Thanks for connecting. I help law firms like [Firm Name] cut the time spent on client intake and follow-up using AI automations — typically saves 5–10 hours a week without adding headcount.

Curious — is manual admin something that's on your radar at the moment, or is it reasonably under control?
Message 3 — If They Engage / Reply
Good to know. The firms I work with usually get the most value from automating one of three things: new client intake, follow-up sequences, or CRM data entry.

Worth a 20-minute call to see if any of those are costing you time? No pitch — just a quick workflow audit. Happy to share the link if useful.
Message Sequence — IFAs
Message 1 — Connection Request
Hi [First Name] — I work with IFA practices on automating the admin and client communication side of the business. Would be great to connect with people in the space.
Message 2 — After They Accept
Thanks for connecting. I build automations for IFA firms — mainly around client onboarding, annual review prep, and CRM workflows. The goal is to cut admin time without touching compliance.

Is admin overhead something that's a friction point in your practice currently?
Message 3 — If They Engage
The two things that save IFA firms the most time in my experience are automating the onboarding paperwork sequence and getting CRM updates to happen automatically after client calls.

I do free 20-minute workflow audits — happy to map out what's automatable in your practice if you'd find that useful. Want me to send the link?
The Rules That Keep Outreach From Dying
✓
Never pitch in message 1. Ever. A connection request with a pitch has a near-zero acceptance rate.
✓
Ask a question in message 2, don't make a statement. Questions get replies. Statements get ignored.
✓
20 new contacts per week is the minimum. Below that, the pipeline doesn't sustain itself.
✓
If they don't reply to message 2, one follow-up after 5–7 days, then move on. No more than 3 messages total.
✓
Personalise message 1 with the firm name at minimum. Generic blasts get ignored faster than you think.

Weekly KPIs — keep it simple, track it every Friday

The Honest Month-1 Expectation
Month 1 is a pipeline-building month, not a revenue month. If you end April with 5+ active conversations, 2–3 calls completed, and 1 proposal out — that is a successful month. The first client almost always comes from month 2 or 3 as trust compounds. The mistake most people make is judging the strategy too early and switching approaches before it has time to work. Commit to the plan for 60 days before changing anything significant.
