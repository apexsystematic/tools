01 How Fiverr Differs from Upwork

The fundamentals of your positioning carry over. Everything else is different. Read this before touching any copy.

FactorUpworkFiverr
Client intentHas a problem, posts a job, reads proposalsKnows what they want, searches Gigs, buys directly
Your jobWin a competitive proposal processShow up in search and convert on the Gig page
ScopeCustom, negotiated per projectProductised — fixed deliverable, fixed price, fixed timeline
PricingHourly or fixed, openly negotiatedThree tiers (Basic/Standard/Premium) — non-negotiable in public
Copy toneConsultative, professionalPunchy, benefit-led, outcome-first
ReviewsJob Success Score builds over timeReview count and rating visible immediately — critical to early sales
Search rankingBased on profile strength + proposal activityBased on Gig SEO, conversion rate, and response time
Client qualityGenerally higher budget, more sophisticatedMixed — but professional services buyers do exist at $300–800+
The key mindset shift: On Upwork you chase jobs. On Fiverr, jobs come to you — but only if your Gig titles and descriptions are built around how buyers search, not how you describe your service. A buyer doesn't search "Make.com automation specialist." They search "automate my client intake" or "law firm workflow automation." Write for the search, not the CV.

08 Search & Algorithm Strategy

Fiverr's algorithm ranks Gigs based on four things: keyword match, conversion rate, response time, and order completion rate. You control all four.

01
Keywords in the right places Your primary keyword must appear in the Gig title, the first line of your description, and your tags. For Gig 1 that's "client intake automation." For Gig 2 it's "Make.com automation." Don't stuff — use it naturally 3–4 times in the description. Fiverr reads the full text.
02
Response time under 1 hour (during working hours) Fiverr shows your average response time on your profile. Under 1 hour is the target. Set up mobile notifications. Respond to every message even if it's just "Thanks — I'll review this and get back to you within a few hours." Speed signals professionalism and boosts your ranking.
03
Conversion rate — get buyers to click, then close Fiverr tracks how many people view your Gig vs. how many order or message you. Your Gig thumbnail is the primary driver of clicks. Your description is the primary driver of messages. Treat them separately. A bad thumbnail tanks your click rate even if the copy is excellent.
04
Never cancel an order Cancellations damage your ranking more than a 4-star review. If an order arrives that's poorly scoped or outside your capabilities, communicate immediately and request a mutual cancellation explaining the reason. Better still — the "message me first" instruction in your description prevents most of these.
05
Gig thumbnail — treat it like an ad Your thumbnail appears at roughly 280×180px in search results. It needs to communicate your service and niche in under 2 seconds. Use a dark background (consistent with Apex Systematic's visual identity), a short punchy headline ("Automate Your Client Intake"), and the Make.com logo or a simple flow diagram. No stock photos. No generic robot imagery.
09 Getting Your First Reviews

Zero reviews on Fiverr is a harder starting point than zero reviews on Upwork — on Upwork your proposal can compensate. On Fiverr, buyers are making a purchase decision with no conversation. Here's how to break through.

Tactic 1 — Share externally
Post your Gig links on LinkedIn, in relevant Facebook groups (legal tech, IFA communities, small business automation), and in your personal network. Fiverr allows you to fulfil orders from buyers you bring yourself — and those orders still count toward your review total and Level 1 status.
Tactic 2 — Offer a stripped-down first order
If someone in your network needs automation work, fulfil it via Fiverr at the Basic tier price — even if the build is worth more. You want the review on the platform, not just the cash. One review on Fiverr is worth more than three referrals from a client who doesn't leave one.
Tactic 3 — Ask explicitly at delivery
When you mark an order complete, include in your delivery message: "If you're happy with the build, a review on Fiverr makes a huge difference — it only takes a minute and I'd really appreciate it." Direct, not pushy. Fiverr buyers are used to being asked.
Tactic 4 — Buyer requests
Fiverr has a "Buyer Requests" section (now called "Brief & Match" on some accounts) where buyers post what they need. Check it daily in the first month and send custom offers. These leads are warm — they've already stated a need.
Never do this: Don't ask friends or family to place fake orders or leave fabricated reviews. Fiverr's fraud detection is sophisticated and account bans are permanent. One real review from a real client is worth infinitely more than five fake ones and a banned account.
